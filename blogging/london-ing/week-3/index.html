On the Up.

I'm not on any drugs, so I'm not sure what's kept me going at this rate for the past three weeks. Through jet lag and sickness and flat hunting, how am I accomplishing as much as I am? The city rush? Excitement of something new? Adrenaline? My theory is that it's a combination of all these things with a strong helping of supporting characters to push me along - all my flatmates and friends. Cities are really hard places unless you've got some sort of human support network. Meg and Leigh were a great start, especially with the addition of Meg's housemates. But I've learned over the last year how necessary it is to have an extensive network and how valuable it is for me to have a wide variety of strong relationships. Luckily, I've had quite a few weeks before term time begins for my programme. Time and determination are the most important ingredients to building a friend foundation in a new city. I've got both.

I've also got a new flatmate who is great for professional connections. Gintare's boyfriend is our fifth flatmate for a little while. He's just got a job in London and doesn't start until October, about the same time that I do. When we met on Monday night, we agreed to be exploring pals. Here is a list of how our week has gone:

And these were only the major events I remember now. A seriously full week. PLUS, I hung out with my Irish American bestie Friday night. Caitlin and I were tag-alongs at a travel blogger meetup. We connected on the fact that we were both plus ones, and I surprised myself with how excited I was to meet another American. I thought I was going to shun other Americans and focus on the Brits, but there was something comforting about hearing my accent in someone else's voice. Since then, I've gotten over that novelty. Caitlin and Maggie (my American flatmate) are all the American I need and I can focus on the international friendships now.
