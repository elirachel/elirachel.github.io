And Down.

After that last optimistic and energetic post, I'll be honest and say that I crashed this week. Especially at the beginning of the week, I didn't go very far from my home. It's been nice to walk around my neighborhood though. My Oyster card ran out of gas on Sunday and I didn't even try to refill it until Thursday - that's how dedicated I was to walking everywhere. I've mentioned this before, but I really have so much faith in walking as a means of knowing your neighborhood. Of course, I did spend a few days this week not going any farther than my neighborhood Sainsburys, not very exploratory.

A few fun things I did include tagging along with a kite surfing club with an LSE sports club. Elliott asked if I wanted to fly kites in Hyde park. I of course said I would. I didn't realize until after I'd committed that he was speaking of an organized event with a kite as long as a Mini Cooper. It was a fun time and we made a few friends. He's planning on going back to wakeboard with them, but I've never been consistent with sports groups and don't plan on starting now. I also had a great time with Caitlin again this weekend. We went out dancing Friday night and lamented the next morning over the lack of delicious, accessible breakfast options in London (i.e. NYC).

With my sudden drop in energy, I started thinking about what "settling in" really entails. What actions are associated with that? Why do I have the idea that getting comfortable means saying no? I can't tell if it's good or bad. Probably, I would find that the pros and cons balance each other the way they do when I'm on the road and purposely unsettled. It's just a different way of living, I guess.

This post feels unfinished, but I don't have anything else to say on this topic right now. I'm sure partly due to my lack of energy and partly to the unfinished thoughts and questions I have.

xo, Eli
