Portland or Bust

I spent much of today playing with babies. One of them spent a few hours trying to launch himself off the steps of a concrete patio. [insert concrete baby photo] Another started to get upset and decided that yoga was an appropriate way to respond. [insert yoga baby photo] Yet another stuck his head under this little door; he was really trying to get through, just thought it was cool to put his head under a door. [insert door baby photo]

No, I don't know any families here, so it is reasonable for you to ask where I found these babes. What kind of parents are handing their kids off to a nomadic stranger?

While in Portland, I'm a temporary nanny. When I was "planning" my month here, I was expecting to sustain myself with my savings and maybe some online tutoring. Perhaps a job at a restaurant here if I felt I really needed to. I spent a little time on Care.com looking for some childcare gigs, but not many people are looking for a nanny for the one month of April. There was only one family that I found. I applied and got the best response from them I could have hoped for, though I didn't know it at the time. They had already put down a deposit for a daycare, unfortunately, BUT they liked my application so much that they forwarded it to another family and recommended me to NorthWest Nannies.

I applied to be a temporary nanny with NWN and heard back from them within a week. After a quick conversation and a phone interview, I had a job in Portland for the month! They were so professional and proactive about hiring me and setting me up with jobs. I had my first gig only a few days after I arrived and my second, third, fourth, and fifth before the end of my first full week. I am excited about this for many reasons.

I’ve driven this way before with my bud, Emily. May 2014, right after I graduated, we started in New York, took a quick tour out to some southwestern states, and looped back around to my family in Oklahoma. I missed her so much along this journey! Though they were nice enough, I found my Ridester passengers’ company to be much less stimulating than hers was.

Actually, after we dropped J off in Boise, I got really sick of B. Everything he did and said annoyed the tar out of me. Not that he changed at all, necessarily; I was just done being in the car and bored of him, which is my personality more than any fault of his. So, I dropped him off really quick at his hostel and tried to be polite in my farewell.

“Hit me up if you want to hang out before you leave Portland!” he called as he shut the back, passenger-side door.

“Alright! Have a nice life!” I returned as I actually thought, “Hell no way am I contacting you again, Felicia.”

I zipped back over the bridge to my new home in NE Portland. No bust here; I made it to Oregon.
